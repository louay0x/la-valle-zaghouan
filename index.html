The Valley

A table set at the foot of the mountain

Forty minutes from Tunis, the road climbs into the foothills of Jebel Zaghouan and opens onto a quiet plateau of pine, olive and stone. La Vallée des Anges sits here, facing the mountain — a place built for long lunches, slow afternoons, and evenings that don't want to end.

Stone terraces, shaded pergolas and open lawns step down the hillside toward the valley floor, framing the same view that has drawn travellers to Zaghouan for centuries: limestone peaks catching the light, and silence broken only by wind and birdsong.

Experiences

Four ways to spend a day in the valley

From the first coffee on the terrace to a late dinner under string lights — the venue moves with the day, each space designed for a different rhythm.

Morning

The Café

An open-air café terrace for slow mornings — Tunisian coffee, fresh orange juice, pastries and breakfast plates, taken in the cool mountain air with the valley still in shadow.

Midday

The Pool & Gardens

A panoramic swimming pool set into the hillside, surrounded by sun loungers, palms and lawn. Built for long afternoons — swim, then rest in the shade with the mountain in full view.

Evening

The Restaurant

Tunisian and Mediterranean cuisine served on a stone terrace facing the sunset — grilled meats and fresh seafood, seasonal produce from the valley, and a wine list chosen to match the view.

Night

Events & Celebrations

Weddings, engagements, corporate evenings and private parties hosted across the gardens and main hall — full event planning, catering and decor, built around the same mountain backdrop.

Stay

Wake up facing the mountain

A small number of guest rooms sit at the quiet edge of the property, away from the terraces — simple, comfortable, and built for travellers who want to wake up early and watch the sun rise over Jebel Zaghouan.

Each room opens onto its own patch of garden, with breakfast served on the café terrace and easy access to the pool, restaurant and grounds throughout your stay.
